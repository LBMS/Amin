About Us


Louis Braille Memorial School for the sightless is a name in the world of education for the visually and otherwise handicapped in West Bengal. This school started its activities officially in 1977 with certain basic aims.

This school is from Nursery to Class X. It is Co Educational and residential. This is the only residential school for Blind girls in Punjab State of India. School provides totally FREE education to blind boys and girls. No fees of any kind is charged for education, uniform, stationary, meals & hostels.

School has the latest equipment for the Blind children, including Read IT Wand, which can scan a book, newspaper or magazine and read it aloud to the Blind students. It is easy to operate and Blind friendly.
they are :-

  1. Transmission of education to the sightless.

  2. Economic Rehabilitation of the sightless.

  3. Ensuring social security.

  4. Ensuring good physical and mental health for visually, otherwise and multiple handicapped person through various physical cultures, sports and games.

  5. Research on continuous development of handicapped people.

  6. Steps towards fight against eradication of blindness..

  7. Last but not least, infuse feeling of self respect amongst handicapped people . Gradual growth of this institution has a long history of struggle. Since 1973-74 ,Pankaj Kumar Das , the then treasure of Blind Personsâ€™ Association surveyed and toured through the villages of the district Hooghly and other surrounding districts of west Bengal tirelessly. This was a mission to arouse awareness amongst rural people that Blind boys and girls are no burden to the society. They could be educated , trained and could serve the society in the same way as a sighted persons. People responded to the call of Sri Das. But there was no School for the blind persons in and around this district


This dynamic personality Pankaj Kumar Das took the venture of establishing this organization under the banner of Blind Persons Association. As a first step he along with a few associates door to door coaching classes in remote villages.

This group of dynamic blind person having a definite conviction, continued collection of fund, contacts with people of different level of the society and formed a committee for establishing this school.

The journey which started through door to door contacts took a definite step forward on 16th October , 1977, where Dr. Sushil Kumar Mukhopadhyay, the then vice chancellor of University of Calcutta inaugurated the school at Makhla, at an open land (by the side of Makaltola Sporing Club) donated by Sm Manju Chakraborty, a resident of Nabagram Konnagra .

Hostel
The School is having two separate hostel buildings for girls and boys in the school campus only. The hostel facility is provided free of cost.Two male wardens for boys and one lady warden are working full time to take care of blind students. All the rooms are equipped with four beds with mattresses and pillows, separate almirahs, one harmonium, one tape recorder to listen audio cassettes and fans etc.
Music
The music subject is compulsory for class 1st to 8th and as an optional subject for class 9th & 10th under which the training is given for all instruments like Guitar, Harmonium, Tabla, Drum, Piano, flute and Congo etc by trained music teachers. The school is having its own Orchestra party who use to perform special programmes on the eve of Republic day, Independence day, official functions like Braille day, VIP/ donor visit to school and on various other occasions. Many awards have been received for their outstanding cultural program at National and District level.
Library
There is one big library hall in the school containing more than 6500 Braille books of all types including text books of course material, competitive exams books, fiction/novels, story books, holy books like Ramayana, Geeta, Mahabharata, Quran and Bible etc and other various reference books for classes 1st to 12th & higher education also. School subscribes various fortnightly / monthly / quarterly magazines and Journals useful for blind students and teachers for their updation of current affairs & general knowledge.
